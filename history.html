Club History

Over a century of crown green bowls in the heart of Willenhall

A chronological record of Willenhall Trinity Bowls Club since its founding in 1921. If you have any corrections or further information please get in touch.

1921

The playing fields in Charles Street, Sandbeds were bought on behalf of Russell Street Methodist Church for £310 on June 29th. J.T. Kidson, H.H. Griffiths, R.E. Bailey, F. Smith, C. Griffiths, G. Hunt and E. Downing loaned the purchase money free of interest for 2 or 3 years.

The church minute book records: "that the land purchased be conveyed to J.T. Kidson, H.H. Griffiths and Caleb Griffiths in trust for the sole use and benefit of Russell Street Society."

Taken from "The Story of a Hundred Years 1850–1950" by George H. Parbrook.

1936

Club entered teams in the Willenhall League and the Church & Chapel League.

1937

E. Dainty of Willenhall Prims won the Staffordshire Individual Merit.

1940

Caleb Griffiths passed away on 24th May.

1942

A second team was entered in the Willenhall League.

1946

H.H. Griffiths passed away on October 9th.

1949

F. Stringer of Willenhall Prims won the Staffordshire Individual Merit. E. Stringer volunteered as new Vice President after a new County ruling required clubs to have one before entering the Staffordshire Cup.

1952

First Presentation Evening held at the British Restaurant on Thursday October 16th.

1961

Staffordshire Consolation Cup — Willenhall Prims lost by 35 shots to Amery Unionist A on Willenhall Liberal green.

First AGM Supper held on March 13th at the Neptune Public House.

1965

With the amalgamation of various churches it was decided to change the name of the club from Willenhall Prims to Willenhall Trinity.

Willenhall Trinity became the first non-works side to join the Wolverhampton Works League.

1966

The Russell Street Methodist Playing Fields ceased, leaving just the Bowling Club on the original land. From its purchase in 1921, the sports of tennis, football, cricket and putting had taken place there.

1967

New Clubhouse completed. The building was originally on part of barracks on Cannock Chase — it was cut in half, modified and erected in its present position.

1969

The new Clubhouse was paid for. £37-10-0 was available towards the approx £45 needed for "inserting electricity."

1971

Bert Boughey, who had been Secretary of the Club for 35 years, sadly passed away on January 6th.

1977

Club entered a team into the Wolverhampton Senior Citizens League.

1979

The AGM noted this was the League's 50th year. Eaton Yale & Willenhall Prims (Trinity) were the only two clubs with continuous membership. Shed Roberts celebrated 50 years as a member.

1981

Trophy donated by the family in memory of Elroy Squire, plus £100 left in his will. Trophy awarded to the Club Championship Winner.

1985

Members' wives, girlfriends or sisters allowed associate membership at £1-50 (full membership for men was £5), allowing them to play in afternoons.

1988

A team entered into the Cannock Thursday League.

A second score board presented to the Club by Mark Beard.

1989

New electrical supply installed on overhead poles.

Floodlighting installed at a cost of £1,200.

1991

New path laid around the green at a cost of £510.

A second team entered into the Wolverhampton Senior Citizens League.

1994

New gates fitted to the entrance at a cost of £475.

1995

Female relatives (wives, daughters, sisters) of Club members now accepted as full members of the club.

1996

Lady members attended AGM for the first time.

Club reached the quarter final of the Staffordshire Cup.

1998

A team entered into the Walsall Veterans League. Majority of field adjacent to the green sold by the Circuit to Nightfreight.

2000

Sale of Scratch cards introduced to raise funds. John Tew stepped down as captain of the A team after 25 years.

2001

Club rules replaced by Club Constitution. Large hole appeared in the green — Coal Board investigated for a mine shaft but after digging out a 6ft cube found all sides and bottom sound.

New fencing installed by Probation Service at no cost. Second Championship board obtained.

2004

First Bowls Club Choral Concert held at Short Heath Methodist Church — 175 people attended making a profit of £669.

Lottery Bonus Ball Competition started to raise funds. First Sporting Evening held.

2008

Completion of major clubhouse alterations — new underground power supply, disabled toilet, new kitchen, and improved disabled access. Official opening attended by over 100 people on June 20th, with unveiling of commemorative plaques by Club President Eric Shepherd.

First Fun Day held for bowlers, family and friends.

2009

The start of the bowling season was filled with sadness with the loss of our President, Eric Shepherd. Our longest serving member, joining when 16 years old, he died aged 84 — 68 years with the club. He won the Club Championship 7 times and was probably the best and most consistent bowler to have played for the club.

"Eric played bowls as he lived life, in a friendly spirit, generous in defeat and showing humility in victory."

2010

New floodlights installed.

  • Wolverhampton Works League Division 2 Winners & Dunn Cup Winners
  • Cannock Thursday League Division 2 Runners Up
  • Wolverhampton Senior Citizens League Division 3 Runners Up
  • Walsall Veterans League Division 4 Runners Up

Rambling Section started monthly walks.

2012

New B teams entered into Wolverhampton Senior Citizens League & Heath Town Over 50s Leagues.

Walsall Veterans A team Winners of Division 2. Cannock Thursday League Winners of Division 2 League and Cup.

2013

New Visitors enclosure provided — Named Kempscott Enclosure — cost covered by donations from Peter Prescott (Chairman) & Norman Kempson (Secretary).

Wolverhampton Works League Division 2 Winners & Dunn Cup Winners.

2014

New C teams entered in the Wolverhampton Senior Citizens and Heath Town Over 50s Leagues — Club now had 12 teams.

Winners of £3,018 in Express & Star "Cash for your Community" Competition. Automatic Irrigation System purchased.

2015

Automatic Irrigation Scheme installed. New ceiling installed in clubhouse, picture wall created, photographs reframed. New upstands fitted around green.

2016

Stand on top of bank replaced — named John Tew Terrace. Defibrillator purchased for Clubhouse.

Club win Staffs Consolation Cup & Wolverhampton Works League Division 1 for the first time in the Club's history.

Walsall Veterans & Heath Town Over 50s A teams promoted to respective 1st Divisions.

2018

Club win Staffs Consolation Cup for the 2nd time in 3 years.

Wolverhampton Works League team win Chronicle Cup for the first time, as well as winning the Hospital Cup — both finals against Bilston.

The inaugural Ladies Competition for the Bert Williams Decanter.

2019

New team entered into the Bilston LV League.

2020

Four CCTV cameras installed to improve security.

Bowling suspended due to the Coronavirus Pandemic.

2022

🎉 Centenary Year. With the closure of The Yale Green, the remaining Yale team joined Trinity as Trinity A in the Bilston LV League.

Centenary events included:

  • Presidents v Chairmans Bowls Match
  • Toffs v Workers Bowls Match
  • 12-Hour Bowlathon
  • Trinity Through the Decades Talent Show
  • Bowlers Service
  • Centenary Arch and Garden built

Our President John Tew sadly passed away at the age of 92.

2025

Grant of £2,600 awarded from Sport England towards a £5,100 reconditioned Toro mower.
